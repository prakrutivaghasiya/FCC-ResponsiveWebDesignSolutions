Irrfan Khan

One of the Finest Actors in Indian Cinema

Here's a time line of Irrfan Khan's life:

  • 1967 - Born in Tonk, Rajasthan, India
  • 1984 - completes his MA in Jaipur before joining the National School of Drama (NSD) in New Delhi in 1984 to study acting
  • 1987 - was offered a minor role in Mira Nair's Salaam Bombay!
  • 1995 - Khan married writer and fellow NSD graduate Sutapa Sikdar.
  • 2001 - Asif Kapadia cast him as the lead in The Warrior, a historical film completed in 11 weeks on location in Himachal Pradesh and Rajasthan
  • 2003 - acted in Ashvin Kumar's short film, Road to Ladakh; the film received rave reviews at international festivals.[35] That same year, he played the title role in the critically acclaimed Maqbool, an adaptation of Shakespeare's Macbeth.
  • 2004 - won the Filmfare Best Villain Award for his role in Haasil.
  • 2005 - His first Bollywood lead role came in 2005 with Rog. His performance was praised by critics; one wrote, "Irfaan's eyes speak louder than his words and every time he is in frame, be it talking to his buddy Manish or arguing with Suhel, he shows his capability as an actor".
  • 2006 - He went on to gain critical acclaim for his roles in The Namesake
  • 2007 - Life in a... Metro
  • 2008 - Khan played a police inspector in the 2008 film Slumdog Millionaire, for which he and the cast of the movie won Screen Actors Guild Award for Outstanding Performance by a Cast in a Motion Picture
  • 2011 - Was awarded the Padma Shri, India's fourth highest civilian honour, Won the National Film Award for Best Actor for portraying title character in Paan Singh Tomar
  • 2012 - changed his first name from "Irfan" to "Irrfan"; he said he liked the sound of the extra "r" in his name.
  • 2015 - was appointed the brand ambassador for "Resurgent Rajasthan", a campaign by the state government of Rajasthan.
  • 2017 - Khan's portrayal as Raj Batra in Hindi Medium earned him several accolades, one of which was the Filmfare Award for Best Actor. Hindi Medium became a sleeper hit in India and China, becoming his highest-grossing Hindi release, surpassing The Lunchbox.
  • 2018 - he had been diagnosed with a neuroendocrine tumor. He sought treatment in UK for a year and returned India in February 2019
  • 2020 - was admitted to Mumbai's Kokilaben Dhirubhai Ambani Hospital on 28 April 2020, where he started receiving treatment for a colon infection. Khan died the following day at age 53, from the infection
